§ 4101. Definitions
In this chapter:(1) Delivery order contract.—The term “delivery order contract” means a contract for property that—(A) does not procure or specify a firm quantity of property (other than a minimum or maximum quantity); and

(B) provides for the issuance of orders for the delivery of property during the period of the contract.


(2) Task order contract.—The term “task order contract” means a contract for services that—(A) does not procure or specify a firm quantity of services (other than a minimum or maximum quantity); and

(B) provides for the issuance of orders for the performance of tasks during the period of the contract.


(Pub. L. 111–350, § 3, Jan. 4, 2011, 124 Stat. 3778.)
