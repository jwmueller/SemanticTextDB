§ 6561. Abolition of United States International Development Cooperation Agency(a) In generalExcept for the components specified in subsection (b) of this section, the United States International Development Cooperation Agency (including the Institute for Scientific and Technological Cooperation) is abolished.


(b) AID and OPIC exemptedSubsection (a) of this section does not apply to the Agency for International Development or the Overseas Private Investment Corporation.


(Pub. L. 105–277, div. G, subdiv. A, title XIV, § 1411, Oct. 21, 1998, 112 Stat. 2681–790.)

Effective DatePub. L. 105–277, div. G, subdiv. A, title XIV, § 1401, Oct. 21, 1998, 112 Stat. 2681–790, provided that: 
“This title [see Tables for classification], and the amendments made by this title, shall take effect on the earlier of—
“(1) April 1, 1999; or

“(2) the date of abolition [Apr. 1, 1999] of the United States International Development Cooperation Agency pursuant to the reorganization plan described in section 1601 [22 U.S.C. 6601].”
